About

Our goal is not to change people's perspective. In business, the general rule is to not change consumer's perspective but follow them or build upon them. Life is, sometimes unfortunately, business.
Andrew C. Wang

Donate Anything started off on a wall full of sticky notes. It was an idea that came up during the COVID-19 pandemic when I realized how terrible my disposal habits have become. Not only because of the face masks (which you should be disposing) and one-use items (including food!), but the lack of consciousness about wasteful habits.

Besides the usual plastic cartons that I had to wash and uncap, I really didn't know how much and which items I should be recycling. I grew too tolerant to the mindset of "someone else will take care of it," so to the recycling bin ya' go. Same with Blue Cross donations. People keep tossing old, dirty textiles into the "Donation" bin, expecting Blue Cross to just accept such blessings.

So... this website tries to solve this problem?

After that entire talk, the answer is "no." Unlike the past, people express their freedom vibrantly in words and inaction. For me, the only societal solution that came to mind was "re-routing" the problem.

How can you make someone feel good? The act of kindness through indirect, materialistic givings, of course. The problem we're re-routing is waste disposal, mostly the "mindset" of disposal. In the U.S., a large grey truck appears every week, grabs your trash, and everything magically disappears. Worries gone, right?

People are not good at long term thinking, and we're not aiming to change everyone's wasteful habits. We can only do so much to improve ourselves.
